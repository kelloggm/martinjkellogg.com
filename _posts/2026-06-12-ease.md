Our paper [Verifier Warnings Do Not Improve Comprehensibility Prediction](https://kelloggm.github.io/martinjkellogg.com/papers/ease-rene-2026-preprint.pdf)
appeared at the Reproducibility and Negative Results track of EASE. Though this works shows that adding verifier warnings to the state-of-the-art experimental
ML models for comprehensibility doesn't make them better, our results actually suggest that the fundamental problem is that those state-of-the-art models
just aren't very good (as the authors of the papers we're working on top of concluded), and verifier warnings just aren't _enough_ to fix the problem.
More work on this topic coming soon, but you can look at [this arxiv paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.03474) if you'd like a hint ;)
